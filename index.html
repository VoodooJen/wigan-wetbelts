    The Hidden Threat

    Your wet belt is silently destroying your engine.

    Wet timing belts run inside the engine, bathed in oil. As they age, they shed rubber particles that block the oil pickup screen, starving your engine of pressure. Catch it early and it's a belt change. Leave it, and it's a new engine.

    Stage 1

    Wet Belt Degradation

    Rubber compound breaks down in modern fuels and oil. Microscopic particles begin shedding into the sump.

    Stage 2

    Oil Pickup Blockage

    Belt debris collects on the pickup screen, choking the oil supply at exactly the moment your engine needs it most.

    Stage 3

    Low Oil Pressure

    Pressure drops below spec at idle. By the time the warning light comes on, the damage is already underway.

    Stage 4

    Timing Failure

    A skipped or snapped belt sends pistons into valves at full speed. Catastrophic, immediate, expensive.

    Stage 5

    Engine Damage

    Bearing wear, scored cams, bent valves. Repair bills climb past £4,000, often into full engine replacement.

    The Fix

    Early Replacement

    A scheduled wet belt service costs a fraction of a new engine. Book in before your interval and protect your investment.
